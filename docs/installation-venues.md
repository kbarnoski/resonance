# Resonance Installation — Target Venues

A prioritized list of venues to send `installation-brief.md` to. Ordered by realistic likelihood of bite × strategic value, with contact angle and the right ask shape for each.

---

## Tier 1 — strong fit, low barrier (start here)

### 1. Envelop SF
- **Why fit**: Purpose-built spatial audio venue, Christopher Willits' programming explicitly favors slow, contemplative, music-led work. Resonance is in their wheelhouse.
- **Contact path**: hello@envelop.us · DM Christopher Willits on IG / Twitter for a warmer intro.
- **Ask**: One-night listening event, 60–90 min program (two ~30 min Paths back-to-back). Easy yes if it lands.

### 2. National Sawdust (Brooklyn)
- **Why fit**: Experimental new music venue, programs immersive AV alongside concerts; their Spring/Fall seasons regularly include installation-format pieces. Paola Prestini (founder) is a composer — same wavelength.
- **Contact path**: programming@nationalsawdust.org · Paola Prestini directly via website intro form.
- **Ask**: Slot in their immersive series, or a residency week leading to a public showing.

### 3. The Lab (San Francisco, Mission)
- **Why fit**: Sound art + experimental visual programming. Local, smaller scale, exactly the kind of venue that takes risks on new contemplative work.
- **Contact path**: dena@thelab.org (Dena Beard, ED) · open submissions on website.
- **Ask**: One-week run, public on weekends. Very achievable for a first installation.

### 4. Issue Project Room (Brooklyn)
- **Why fit**: Sound-art residencies are their core. They host long-form installations and have a reclining-listening tradition.
- **Contact path**: opportunities@issueprojectroom.org · their Artist Salon submission window.
- **Ask**: Residency culminating in a public installation week.

## Tier 2 — planetarium / science-museum tradition

### 5. Morrison Planetarium (California Academy of Sciences, SF)
- **Why fit**: Local, full-dome projection, has hosted experimental "Fulldome" art programming after-hours (e.g., NightLife events, special screenings). Closer to the literal "planetarium vibes" frame.
- **Contact path**: planetarium@calacademy.org · also worth checking their NightLife programmer.
- **Ask**: After-hours fulldome show. Requires adapting visuals to dome geometry — separate technical lift but tractable.

### 6. Hayden Planetarium / AMNH (NYC)
- **Why fit**: Same tradition as Morrison, larger audience. They program experimental dome content occasionally.
- **Contact path**: through AMNH press / programming office; harder cold-pitch — would benefit from a curatorial intro.
- **Ask**: Likely a one-night experimental program, not a run.

### 7. Adler Planetarium (Chicago) — *Definiti dome AV programming*
- **Why fit**: They've shown contemporary fulldome art (e.g., the *Solarium* series). More open than the coastal-museum tier.
- **Contact path**: programming@adlerplanetarium.org.
- **Ask**: Same as Morrison — fulldome show.

## Tier 3 — multidisciplinary art spaces (residency potential)

### 8. Pioneer Works (Brooklyn)
- **Why fit**: Multidisciplinary, has the gallery shell + technical infrastructure for installation-format AV work. Their Music Residency program is composer-friendly.
- **Contact path**: residencies@pioneerworks.org · best to apply during open call (usually Spring).
- **Ask**: Music Residency → ends with public showing. 2–3 month commitment.

### 9. MASS MoCA (North Adams, MA)
- **Why fit**: Massive scale, time-based art friendly, residencies that lead to long-form installation. Sol LeWitt building's contemplative posture matches.
- **Contact path**: studios@massmoca.org (Studios at MASS MoCA program).
- **Ask**: Studio residency culminating in a smaller public installation.

### 10. Wave Hill (Bronx) — *Sunroom Project Space*
- **Why fit**: Their Sunroom Project Space commissions contemplative work; the institution's whole identity is slow-attention-in-public.
- **Contact path**: arts@wavehill.org · open call yearly.
- **Ask**: Sunroom commission. Outdoor/conservatory adjacency makes it interesting.

## Tier 4 — festivals (good for visibility, harder to get in)

### 11. MUTEK (Montréal / Mexico City / Tokyo / SF)
- **Why fit**: New-media + electronic music festival; runs an A/V section. They program installation works alongside live performance.
- **Contact path**: open call (usually Jan–March for the Aug Montréal edition); also direct pitch to local Director (SF: mutek.org/sf).
- **Ask**: A/V program slot + on-site installation.

### 12. CTM Festival (Berlin)
- **Why fit**: Sound art + experimental music, programs installations across the city during festival week.
- **Contact path**: open call yearly, plus direct curator email.
- **Ask**: Festival-period installation in a partner venue.

### 13. Sonar+D (Barcelona)
- **Why fit**: AV / new-tech track, programs installation work.
- **Contact path**: open call.
- **Ask**: Sonar+D installation slot.

---

## Tier 5 — major museums (long shot, need a curatorial relationship)

### 14. SFMOMA — Public Knowledge / Open Space programs
- **Why fit**: They commission new-media and sound work; closer than the other major museums.
- **Contact path**: needs curatorial intro — start by attending a Public Knowledge event in SF.
- **Ask**: Long-term — a commissioned project. Not a cold pitch.

### 15. The Shed (NYC)
- **Why fit**: Built explicitly for experimental commissions across disciplines.
- **Contact path**: same — curatorial intro required, near-impossible cold.
- **Ask**: Commission. Years-long arc.

### 16. MOMA / The Whitney
- **Why fit**: Aspirational. Don't pitch yet. Build the resume first.

## What to send

For Tier 1: just the brief (`installation-brief.md`) + 2–3 stills + a 60–90 second video. Don't overpitch.

For Tier 3 (residencies): the brief + a CV/bio + 1-page project description with proposed timeline and outcomes. Their applications usually have specific prompts — answer those, attach the brief as supporting.

For Tier 4 (festivals): their open-call format is highly structured (Submittable, ad-hoc PDFs). Adapt the brief into the form they want; never send an unformatted PDF when they've asked for specific fields.
