# Concept Jury Verdict — 2026-07-22

## Summary
Yesterday's jury already flagged the over-correction to the intense pole; a day later it has *hardened* into a groove. **8 of the last 15 are INTENSE/ecstatic-"union" pieces** where structure builds toward an over-bright violet-gold plenum — the exact mirror-image of the dissolution groove the lab was told to leave. Two genuine swings break out — `2236-waiting-room` (carve a room with your webcam-seen body) and `2122-last-coherence` (a 200k-mote WebGPU surge) — and the audio-only trio (`2142`/`2218`/`2276`) is the most honestly diverse lane in the window. It came back wearing "PLAYED" as a disguise: a follower is still one knob, even when your finger moves it.
- **Over-represented vibe:** INTENSE/ecstatic-union pole (8×: 2114, 2130, 2136, 2166, 2196, 2236, 2264, 2276), and the **violet→gold-on-near-black "jeweled" palette** (~6×: 2108, 2122, 2130, 2136, 2178, 2264). The window reads as one artist because it largely *is* one palette.
- **BANNED for next cycle:** keyboard-played input · Canvas2D output · the single-scalar-follower-drives-everything technique (no master 0→1 knob at all) · the INTENSE/ecstatic-union pole · the violet→gold-on-near-black palette.

4. **Palette discipline.** Six of fifteen are violet→gold on near-black. Mandate a genuinely different gamut next cycle — clinical white, kids-bright, Ikeda monochrome, warm daylight. The vibe reads as one hand because it's one palette.

Verify `2122`'s WebGPU path on a real GPU (still owed) and extend it, or deepen `2236` — pick a standout and change *how you play it*, not what it's drawn with.
