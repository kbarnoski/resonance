## Standouts (positive)
- **888-living-reverie**: the build of the window and the only 5/5. A real
  long-form spine — a two-tier memory engine (local pitch-cell bank + global
  irreversible 10-minute age/state-machine) that re-voices Karel's own motifs
  across a sparse→bloom→dense→dissolve arc that *cannot return*. This is the
  "5+ minute piece with state and memory" the menu has begged for, built with a
  cited 2026 research basis and two banked deepening siblings. The lab finally has
  a cinematic register, not just toys.
- **883-negative-space**: the cleverest *idea* in the window and the first real
  hit on the conceptual/critical menu category. Every mic instrument ever maps
  loud→active; this inverts it — **silence blooms the music, any sound ducks it to
  nothing.** You compose by what you withhold. Cage's *4′33″* made playable.
  Genuinely novel inversion, not a re-skin.
- **896-tonnetz-loom**: "walk the geometry of harmony." The Euler/neo-Riemannian
  lattice as a tappable SVG where P/L/R each glide exactly one voice in true just
  intonation, your path exportable as vector. A frontier-technique build (Tonnetz
  is 0× prior) that's also legible and complete.
- **902-harmonic-mirror**: the deterministic, zero-latency cousin of an AI
  accompanist — play a few notes and it *completes the chord you imply* in pure
  JI, glide-retuning as your root moves. The first deliberately MIDI/desk piece in
  a while and squarely on Karel's "workspace for composers" line.
- **The citation turnaround itself** is a standout. 877→898 each cite a dated
  arXiv/PMC entry *in the README*; this is the lab responding to a standing jury
  ask at scale, not in one token build.
