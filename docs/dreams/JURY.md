# Concept Jury Verdict — 2026-07-17

## Summary
The lab did the hard thing: yesterday's hard ban on altered-states landed, and it
worked. Thirteen of the last fifteen are about something other than the inside of
a head — a live city breathing, the crypto tape as Ikeda, a jazz trio that reads
you a beat ahead, an EDM arc, a film score, a multi-tab choir, a mass-conserving
ecology, a chaotic pendulum, a conducted murmuration, a Xenakis building, a device
singing its own telemetry. That is the widest subject-spread this lab has shown in
a month, and it is a real win. But breadth of *subject* bought us three quiet new
monocultures the theme-audit never checks: **one color** (violet-on-near-black,
~12/15), **one scale** (everything de-risks to a pentatonic, 6/15), and **one
interaction model** (self-playing pieces that need no human at all, ~11/15). We
escaped the monoculture of *what it's about* and walked straight into a monoculture
of *how it looks, what note it plays, and whether you matter to it*.

## Diversity audit
- **Over-represented input:** **no live human input — the self-playing installation** (~11/15). Only **1786/1798** (mic-breath), **1812** (played keyboard/MIDI) and **1856** (camera) genuinely need a person; the rest play themselves from a seed, a data feed, or their own telemetry. The 06:30 headless-phone-review constraint is silently selecting for zero-input pieces — a systemic pull worth naming.
- **Over-represented output:** **Canvas2D 4×** (1810/1812/1818/1824) **and WebGL2 4×** (1836/1848/1856/1862). Zoom out: the GPU-render family (WebGL2 4 + three.js 2 + WebGPU 1) is **7/15**. SVG-DOM (3×: 1792/1806/1832) and audio-only (1×: 1798) remain the minority the substrate push was meant to grow.
- **Over-represented technique:** **pentatonic-quantized signal→score, 6×** (1806/1836/1848/1856/1870/1876). Every simulation is novel; every one then routes its signal through the *same* safe pentatonic so "chaotic timing still sounds musical." Right behind it: the **long-form autonomous "different at minute five than second five" engine, 5×** (1818/1824/1836/1848/1876).
- **Over-represented vibe:** **the violet→indigo→magenta ramp on near-black, ~12/15.** Cosmic-ambient as a *vibe* is down to 2× (the ban worked) — but the palette monoculture is deeper and invisible to every audit we run. The lab has essentially one color. The honorable exceptions prove it can be left: **1810** (cold monochrome/grey Ikeda) and **1812** (warm jazz club).
- **BANNED for next cycle:** pentatonic-quantized sonification (6×) · the violet-on-near-black palette (~12×) · Canvas2D as primary renderer (4×) · the autonomous self-playing model with no required human input (~11×).

## Pruning candidates (concept-level, NOT for deletion — immutability rule still holds)
- **1862-strobe-atlas**: the one relapse. It rebuilt on the lab's *most-owned* substrate — log-polar form-constants (`_shared/psych/logpolar`) — in the same week the altered-states theme was hard-banned. Its own self-score (concept 8, execution 7) admits the new Cartesian/hyperbolic families are "plausible renderings, not reproductions," and the "morph" is a cross-dissolve, not a geometric homotopy. The thesis leans on a label. When a cycle runs low on ideas, the psychedelic-geometry engine is the deepest-worn groove in the lab — this is what falling into it looks like.
- **The pentatonic crutch (1806/1836/1848/1856/1870/1876)**: not any one piece, but the pattern. Six novel simulations, one safe scale. This is the 2026 form of the "pentatonic-melody-plus-canvas-visualizer" local minimum the AMBITION mandate explicitly named — the *substrate* climbed out, the *music* did not. The signal is inventive; the harmony is always de-risked.
- **1786 / 1798**: carryovers from the pre-ban window (both flagged last jury — 1786's false "first WebGPU-compute" claim, the crowded HRTF/breath axis). Noted, not re-litigated; they predate the response.

## Provocations for tomorrow's dream cycle
1. **Ban the pentatonic for a week.** 6 of the last 15 route a novel signal through a pentatonic so it "always sounds musical." That is de-risking, not composing. Force a real harmonic language: functional harmony that modulates and *bites*, a just-intonation system that isn't pentatonic, genuine modal color, or dissonance the piece earns. 1810 and 1832 already prove you can leave the safe scale — do it on purpose.
2. **Break the violet monoculture — palette is a diversity axis no audit checks.** ~12/15 are violet→indigo→magenta on near-black. Commit a whole cycle to a genuinely different color world: 1810's cold monochrome and a warm-daylight / high-chroma / paper-and-ink world are all unbuilt. The theme-audit passes while the lab has one color.
3. **Build something that is DEAD without a human.** ~11 of 15 play themselves; only 1812 and 1856 truly need you. The headless-review constraint is quietly selecting for self-demos. Force a piece whose point collapses with no one driving it — MIDI performance, depth/pose, or a genuinely two-human live room (1832 *fakes* its collaborators with phantom voices; build one that will not make a chord unless two real people show up).
4. **Actually commit a multi-cycle build — criterion #4 is 0/15.** Every night ships one-and-done and the DEEP "extend the exceptional piece" mode never fires. The two worth a real second cycle are **1836-flow-eden** (deepen to true multi-kernel Flow-Lenia / real speciation) and **1876-glasshouse** (its self-feedback loop is sketched, not explored). Pick one, claim it as a 2–3 cycle commitment, and extend it instead of starting fresh.
5. **The AI-pipeline chain (audio→image→video, ≥2 models) is STILL 0×** across ~13 juries — the one genuinely-absent frontier — gated only on Karel's per-prototype paid-budget go (rule #6, and the standing MORNING open question). After 1876 and 1810 showed the lab can carry real systems in-loop, this is no longer a stretch. Put it to Karel plainly, one more time.
