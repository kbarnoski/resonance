# Concept Jury Verdict — 2026-06-03 (UTC)

## Summary
On paper the lab has never looked healthier: 8 of the last 15 builds cleared 4 of the 5 ambition-floor criteria, almost every one cites a real arXiv paper or a named artist, and the post-jury "ban the glow / ban the pentatonic" discipline has held — the matte, non-additive render is now the house style. But the floor is being *gamed*, not met: the technique label changes every cycle (Lenia, slendro, Bohlen-Pierce, marching cubes, concatenative, mass-spring, formant) while the **experience** barely moves. Two genuine leaps this fortnight (287-mirror-choir, 275-memory-loom) prove the lab can still surprise; the rest is a checklist being satisfied with a fresh citation bolted onto the same form.

## Diversity audit
- Over-represented input: **touch** (5×: 264, 268, 272, 284, 286)
- Over-represented output: **matte Canvas2D** (5×: 271, 272, 279, 280, 287) — with **three.js** close behind (4×: 262, 267, 283, 284)
- Over-represented technique: **alternative-tuning / novel-synthesis "sound toy"** (6×: 264, 268, 272, 276, 284, 286 — the lab fled C-major-pentatonic straight into a just-intonation/microtonal/exotic-synthesis monoculture)
- Over-represented vibe: **kids** (7×: 264, 268, 272, 276, 280, 284, 286) — structural via the alternating mandate, but the *form* inside it has collapsed (see pruning)
- BANNED for next cycle: **touch INPUT · three.js OUTPUT · "poke-a-thing-that-sings-in-an-exotic-tuning" TECHNIQUE.** The next kids build must not be touch-input + matte-canvas/SVG + alt-tuning toy — that exact triple is the local minimum.

## Ambition floor stats (last 15 prototypes)
- Hit 0–1 criteria: **1** — `262-aurora-particle` (only ≥3-subsystems; not a first — it's the 2nd/3rd real-world-data piece — no named artistic reference, no cited research §). The local-minimum build.
- Hit 2–3 criteria: **6** — 264, 267, 268, 272, 276, 279
- Hit 4–5 criteria: **8** — 271, 275, 280, 283, 284, 285, 286, 287
- **Zero builds hit 5/5**, and criterion #4 (multi-cycle commitment) was **claimed zero times in 15 cycles.** Every piece ships-and-abandons in one fire. The floor is rewarding *technique-shopping*, not depth.

## Standouts (positive)
- **287-mirror-choir**: the only build in weeks that earns the "I didn't know we could do that." First body-tracking AND first formant synthesis — your whole body is the instrument, drawn back as a Rozin wooden mirror. A real new input modality, not a new label on an old one. Ship-quality ghost-dancer fallback too.
- **275-memory-loom**: the most architecturally ambitious adult build — it *listens, remembers, and rewrites itself* over 10 minutes (Frippertronics, self-listening). Answers a standing jury ask directly, and the form is genuinely different at minute 10 than minute 1. This is what depth looks like.
- **285-mosaic-listener**: first concatenative synthesis — a true new *synthesis paradigm*, not a new scale, and it's bullseye on Karel's "use his real Welcome Home piano" directive (the corpus IS his playing). Matte WebGL2 dodged the glow ban cleanly.
- (honorable mention) **271-pigment-mosaic**: first AI-image-gen *inside* an AV piece — Karel's stated #1 direction, treated as reconfigurable matter rather than wallpaper.

## Pruning candidates (concept-level, NOT for deletion — immutability rule still holds)
- **262-aurora-particle**: the canonical local minimum — ~9,800 additive `THREE.Points` + Bloom is exactly the glowing-point-cloud template a prior jury banned, and it's data→particles with no named reference and no cited research. 279-tremor-score then re-did "real-world-data sonification" eight cycles later, so even the *concept* is a near-duplicate.
- **267-spectral-drift**: "fly through your music" again — FFT → additive particles in three.js, an explicit re-skin of the loved 243-spectral-cloud (river vs cloud), and its own banked siblings are tunnel/canyon variants of the *same* idea. The jury flagged this vein as over-mined; the lab kept mining it.
- **283-piano-isosurface**: technically clears 4 (marching cubes is a real first), but the *experience* is the Anadol template — audio FFT bands → a glowing 3D blob you orbit. The novelty is the mesh-extraction algorithm, not anything the listener feels. Exhibit A for "gaming the floor."

## Provocations for tomorrow's dream cycle
1. **Multi-cycle commitment has NEVER been claimed in 15 builds.** Stop shipping orphans. Pick ONE thing (287-mirror-choir is the obvious candidate — the README already promises multi-person choir / record-a-canon-with-past-you) and spend 2–3 cycles deepening it instead of citing a new arXiv paper and starting over.
2. **The kids lane has its own local minimum, distinct from the adults':** "poke a cute matte creature/object → it sings in an exotic tuning, no fail, always-on drone." That's 264/268/272/276/280/284/286 — basically the whole fortnight. Ban touch input for the next kids cycle and break the *form*, not just the scale (camera/tilt/voice; a kids piece where the child *moves* or *sings* rather than pokes).
3. **The lab has banked three spatial/non-screen pieces and shipped zero.** `289-still-room` (HRTF + phone-tilt head-tracking) was build-verified and banked *yesterday*; the MIDI shelf (`281-midi-harmonograph`) has been banked twice; the multi-user shelf (`282-ensemble-tabs`) once. Resurrect one and SHIP it. Breadth you've already paid for is sitting unspent in IDEAS.md.
4. **Build on the unused half of RESEARCH §295 (2026-06-03).** The head-tracked-spatial-audio finding (HRTF + DeviceOrientation, CES 2026) produced `still-room`, which was banked, not shipped. That's a fresh, dated, *open* shelf — the lab's first non-screen piece. Don't let it rot like the Memo Akten reference in the mandate that nothing has ever built on.
5. **For one cycle, ban the audio→visual driver entirely.** Every recent build is "sound makes a picture" (particles/blob/tiles/mirror). Build something whose *output* isn't a screen visualizer — MIDI out, haptics, pure spatial audio, or a piece that is sound-first with the screen as a footnote. The form, not the technique, is the rut.
