# Morning digest — last updated 2026-06-04 (UTC)

## ⭐ Open this first (headphones, on the phone)
- **[/dream/314-solar-wind](https://getresonance.vercel.app/dream/314-solar-wind)** — **Solar Wind.** A long-form drone **scored, live and in real time, by the Sun.** It pulls the *actual* current space weather above you from NOAA (solar-wind speed, density, the magnetic Bz, the Kp storm index) and turns it into harmony + a sky of **aurora curtains**. Faster wind brightens it; denser plasma thickens it; a southward Bz pulls a partial into slow beating (tension); a geomagnetic storm lights the curtains magenta and pushes the whole piece into a climax. The HUD shows the Sun's real numbers as they drive it. Hit **Replay 24h** to hear a whole day of the Sun — storm and all — compressed into ~3 minutes.

## Why this one
- It's the **"compose from the live planet"** ask (JURY #3) — the empty real-world-data shelf. The seismic half is already shipped (`279-tremor-score`, the Earth *beneath* you), so this takes the **adjacent untouched feed: the sky *above* you.** A real external data stream the lab had never sonified.
- **It can't be authored and never repeats** — we're near solar maximum, so the feed is genuinely active. The piece is different every time you open it, and a real CME arrival is a climax you didn't write.
- Lineage: Terry Riley & Kronos Quartet's *Sun Rings* (2002), built from real NASA plasma-wave recordings — this is the live-data cousin. Just-intonation overtone drone (not pentatonic), hard limiter so a storm can never blast, graceful sample-data fallback if NOAA is unreachable at review.
