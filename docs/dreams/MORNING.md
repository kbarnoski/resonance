## New since yesterday

- **[/dream/149-kids-color-mix](/dream/149-kids-color-mix)** — Color Mix (kids, Cycle 176)
  Three large glowing circles — rose (C3), amber (E3), violet (G3) — in a triangle on a dark
  canvas. **Drag them together.** Overlapping circles mix their colors (screen compositing)
  AND their notes (gain ramps up). When all three converge: bright white glow + C major chord.
  The visual peak and auditory peak are simultaneous. Each circle breathes gently when isolated.
  **Why open this**: it's the first kids prototype where the *relationship between three objects*
  is the music — not tap/drag/hold on one thing. A 4yo discovers color theory and music theory
  as the same spatial interaction. Very satisfying to drag toward the white convergence.

- **[/dream/148-spatial-palette](/dream/148-spatial-palette)** — Spatial Palette (adult, Cycle 175)
  Drag voices on a dark canvas: X = stereo pan, Y = pitch (one row per semitone, C2→C6).
  Pre-placed C major triad. Drag E4 down one row → chord flips "C" → "Cm" live. Scroll to
  control filter brightness + reverb. Double-click to cycle timbre. Long-press to remove.
  **Why open this**: musical interval theory becomes spatial and tactile.
