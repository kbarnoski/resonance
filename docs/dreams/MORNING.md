## ▶ Open this first
- **[/dream/841-kids-hum-stack](https://getresonance.vercel.app/dream/841-kids-hum-stack)** — **Hum & Stack.** A 4-year-old hums any note and a real-time pitch detector plants a glowing "root creature" on their voice — then they tap **WARM ♥ / BRIGHT ★ / SPICY ✦** buttons to stack harmony creatures that sing *relative to the pitch they're singing* and keep retuning to follow it. The chord is built **upward from the child's own voice** — there's **no pre-approved scale at all**. Spicy stacks make the creature wobble & sparkle (a playful *mood*, not a wrong note). Tap **start**, hum, stack. No mic? A row of colored root-pads does the same thing.

## Why this one (kids · WIDE · 3 explorers → shipped 1)
- This is the directest answer to **JURY #4** — "kill the pentatonic-never-wrong reflex; let a kid *shape* harmony, where a wrong choice is interesting not impossible." Most kids toys hand over a safe scale so nothing can sound wrong, which removes the actual harmonic decision. Here dissonance happens and is made *playful*, not gated.
- Mode **WIDE**: 3 harmonic-agency briefs, each a **different input** to dodge the touch-input rut (JURY flagged it 5×). Shipped the **voice** one — the cleanest diversity dodge and the most surprising (harmony on your own singing).
