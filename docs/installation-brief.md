# Resonance — Installation Brief

*A contemplative listening room. Music-led generative visuals, slow time, no interaction required.*

---

## What it is

Resonance is a generative audiovisual instrument. A composed piece of music drives real-time audio-reactive shaders and AI-curated imagery, producing a 20–40 minute slow-moving visual landscape that never repeats verbatim. Originally built for personal listening, it scales naturally to a shared room: the same engine, the same patience, just larger and quieter.

## The room

Start very simple. Planetarium vibes — a darkened space, audience reclining, eyes up.

- A dark rectangular room — anything from 12×12 ft to 30×40 ft
- Floor mats and pillows; no chairs
- Single overhead projection, dome projection, or large wall display
- 4-corner speaker array (stereo as a baseline)
- Visitors enter and leave on their own time. No timed entry, no headphones, no narration

The piece loops. Someone arrives, lies back, watches a phase or two, walks out. Someone else stays for the whole cycle. Both work.

## The piece

A single ~30-minute Path — three to five composed tracks threaded together. Each track triggers its own *journey*: a sequence of phases (Threshold → Expansion → Apex → Return) where audio-reactive shaders and AI-curated imagery shift slowly with the music. No narration. No wall text. Sound and image, slow.

What a visitor experiences:

> Enters a dark room. Lies back. The first phase opens with a single low tone and a near-black field — slow caustics, the faint hint of a mandalic form. Imagery surfaces gradually as the music builds: water, light, figures that resolve and dissolve. Around the apex the room is full of motion and color; then everything settles back toward stillness. The visitor leaves when ready.

## References

This sits in a small canon:

- **Brian Eno, *77 Million Paintings*** — the generative visual companion to ambient music; proof that slow time finds an audience
- **James Turrell, *Skyspaces*** — the dark contemplative room as primary form, no narrative scaffolding
- **Ryoji Ikeda, *test pattern* / *data.matrix*** — sound and light as total sensorium

Where Resonance differs from the AI-image installations now dominating the field (Anadol-flavored generative spectacle): it's *composed* first. Each track is a written piece of music with intent. The visuals serve the music — not the other way around. AI sits inside the journey as a tool; it's not the headline.

## Why now

Slow attention is unfashionable, which is exactly why it's having a moment. Sleep No More-era audiences trained themselves on it. Planetariums are reclaiming cultural foothold (Hayden, Adler, Morrison reopening renovations). Ambient music is mainstream again — Hiroshi Yoshimura's *Music for Nine Post Cards* hit the Billboard charts in 2024, fifty years after release. Resonance fits this current.

It's also unusually low-overhead for the venue: no live performer, no fragile equipment, no consumables. The installation runs itself.

## Who

Resonance is the current shape of a multi-year practice translating composed music into shared visual experience — first as a personal listening tool, then as a small private community of pianists, composers, and contemplative listeners. The installation is the natural next room.
