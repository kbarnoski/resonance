The Application Answers

"What does your company do?"

Resonance lets musicians upload an album, AI builds an immersive visual experience for each track, and they share the whole thing with a single link.

"Why did you pick this idea?"

I'm a pianist who just released a solo album (Welcome Home). I wanted to share it as more than an audio file. Nothing existed that could turn music into a shareable visual experience, so I built it.

"What's your progress?"

Shipped v1.2 in 6 weeks. 200+ WebGL shaders, AI journey generation, shareable album paths, per-device quality tiers, desktop app. Welcome Home is live as a 13-track shareable path — anyone can walk through it from a single URL.

"How will you make money?"

Three lines: $12/mo pro musician tier, $1.5k/yr installation licenses for galleries/venues, B2B education seats for conservatories. Free tier caps at 10 recordings.

"How many users?"

Pre-launch, 2 active users, but the product is fully functional and the demo speaks for itself. YC respects honesty over inflated numbers.

Phase 2

Prepare for the Interview

10-minute Zoom with 2-3 YC partners: 1 min intro, 3 min demo, 6 min rapid-fire.

Questions They WILL Ask

"Who are your users?"

Musicians and composers who record ideas and want to understand + share them. Starting with solo instrumentalists.

"How big is the market?"

50M+ amateur musicians record ideas monthly. $8B in music tools. Zero products in "music as shareable experience."

"Why hasn't this been built?"

Three things just converged: AI imagery at $0.003/frame, client-side analysis via TF.js, share-link culture.

"Unfair advantage?"

I'm the user. Built the entire product in 6 weeks with Claude as my engineering partner. Proof that AI-augmented solo founders can ship at startup speed.

"Beyond musicians?"

Meditation/wellness, art installations, education, sound designers. But musicians first.

"Biggest risk?"

Retention. The social layer (discovery, profiles) is what makes it sticky. That's the next build.
