Resonance — Installation

A contemplative listening room. Generative audiovisual, music-led, slow time. No interaction required.

What it is

A generative audiovisual instrument.

A composed piece of music drives real-time audio-reactive shaders and AI-curated imagery, producing a 20–40 minute slow-moving visual landscape that never repeats verbatim.

Originally built for personal listening. Scales naturally to a shared room: same engine, same patience, just larger and quieter.

The room

Planetarium vibes. Eyes up.

Start very simple. A darkened space, audience reclining, no interaction required.

  • Space12×12 ft to 30×40 ft. Dome geometry welcome.
  • FloorMats and pillows. No chairs.
  • DisplaySingle overhead projection, dome, or large wall display.
  • Audio4-corner speaker array. Stereo as a baseline.
  • FlowVisitors enter and leave on their own time. No timed entry. No headphones. No narration.
The piece

A single ~30 minute Path.

Three to five composed tracks, threaded into one continuous arc. Each track triggers its own journey: a sequence of phases where audio-reactive shaders and AI-curated imagery shift slowly with the music.

01
Threshold
Open in near-silence. Single tone. Near-black field.
02
Expansion
Imagery surfaces. Music builds. Caustics, mandalas, figures.
03
Apex
Full motion and color. The room is loud and alive.
04
Return
Settle back toward stillness. Cycle resolves and begins again.
What a visitor experiences

Enters a dark room. Lies back. The first phase opens with a single low tone and a near-black field — slow caustics, the faint hint of a mandalic form. Imagery surfaces gradually as the music builds: water, light, figures that resolve and dissolve. Around the apex the room is full of motion and color; then everything settles back toward stillness. The visitor leaves when ready.

Where it differs

Composed first.
AI second.

The AI-image installation has become a genre — Anadol-flavored generative spectacle, large-format, often impressive, often hollow.

Resonance is the inverse. Each track is a written piece of music with intent. The visuals serve the music — not the other way around. AI sits inside the journey as a tool. It is not the headline.

Why now

Slow attention is having a moment.

  • Hiroshi Yoshimura's Music for Nine Post Cards hit the Billboard charts in 2024 — fifty years after release. Ambient music is mainstream again.
  • Planetariums are reclaiming a cultural foothold. Hayden, Adler, and Morrison have all renovated and reopened their domes for contemporary programming.
  • Sleep No More–era audiences trained themselves on long-form, low-direction experiences. The patience is back.

Resonance also runs itself: no live performer, no fragile equipment, no consumables. Unusually low overhead for the venue.
