# 2012 — Enigma Drift

`state: illusory-motion / op-art perceptual hallucination · pole: intense`

**The question:** *What if a completely STILL image streamed with motion
generated by the viewer's own visual cortex — a drug-free perceptual
hallucination — and that illusory motion was audible?*

## What it is

A **Leviant "Enigma"** figure rendered entirely in the SVG DOM: concentric
indigo/bone rings crossed by a dense field of hundreds of fine radial spokes.
The figure is (almost) perfectly static, yet the rings appear to **stream and
slowly rotate**. That motion is not on the screen — it is generated inside the
viewer's visual cortex by the interaction of involuntary micro-eye-movements
(microsaccades) with the high-contrast luminance edges of the spokes. It is
strongest in peripheral vision, so it deepens when you fixate the centre dot.

The perceived streaming is then made **audible**: two oscillator banks are tuned
a few Hz apart, and their interference produces an audible amplitude **beat**.
The beat rate follows the perceived streaming speed, so the shimmer you *hear*
tracks the illusory rotation you *see*.

## The illusion → audio mapping

- **Fixation depth** (holding the pointer still near centre; low pointer
  movement + time-since-last-move) → rising ring contrast, spoke shimmer, and a
  faster audible **beat** (~1 → 6 Hz) with a brighter low-pass.
- **Saccade** (a large flick of the pointer) → briefly snaps/resets the illusion
  and drops the beat, mirroring how a real eye movement resets the effect.
- **Slow drift** (<0.2 Hz spoke rotation + ~0.09 Hz luminance drift) → the tiny,
  near-static drift that re-triggers the illusion when adaptation would fade it.
- **Pointer radius** → selects the "active annulus" (nearer rings brighten) and
  raises the drone's **register**.

## Subsystems

1. **SVG-DOM op-art renderer** (`figure.ts`) — concentric `<circle>` annuli in a
   restrained indigo↔bone duotone, plus one `<path>` node carrying up to ~720
   radial spokes (generated once, animated cheaply).
2. **Illusion-driver engine** (`page.tsx`) — a deterministic sub-0.2 Hz drift +
   a stillness/saccade state machine, driven by `requestAnimationFrame`. No
   `Math.random()` / `Date.now()` in the per-frame path.
3. **Beating / difference-tone audio** (`audio.ts`) — two detuned sine banks
   whose interference beats at the perceived streaming rate, softened by a
   low-pass and a feedback delay. A non-lattice interference model.

## How to use

Press **Begin** (a single gesture, to satisfy autoplay policy) to start the
drone — the visual illusion is already running before that, so the piece
self-demos with no input and works headless. Then stare at the centre dot and
hold still to deepen the streaming; flick the pointer to snap it; move outward
to change the active ring and register. Sliders adjust spoke density and ring
count; Mute silences the drone.

## Named references

- Isia Leviant, *Enigma* (1981) — the source figure.
- Donald MacKay's ray figures (1957) — complementary afterimage streaming.
- Akiyoshi Kitaoka — peripheral-drift illusions ("Rotating Snakes").
- Beat / difference-tone perception — interference of two near frequencies.

## Safety

This is deliberately a STILL image. All luminance change is a slow **drift**
well under 0.2 Hz — there is no flicker or strobe, and no full-screen contrast
flashing. `prefers-reduced-motion` softens the drift and luminance amplitude
further.

## Known limits

- Gaze fixation is *approximated* from pointer stillness (no camera / eye
  tracker), so on touch or with an idle mouse the effect simply deepens on its
  own — which is the intended self-demo behaviour.
- The illusion's strength varies per person and is stronger in the periphery of
  a reasonably large display; small windows weaken it.
- If `AudioContext` is unavailable the visual runs silently with a
  `text-destructive` note.
